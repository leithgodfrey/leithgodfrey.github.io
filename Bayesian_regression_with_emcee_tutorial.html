The Jet Power in AGN Campaign (JetPAC)

JetPAC (Jet Power in AGN Campaign) is a new program to accurately measure kinetic powers of relativistic jets in Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN). It involves a combination of conventional and very long baseline (VLB) radio observations, and modeling of different classes of radio AGN. As part of the JetPAC collaboration, we are developing new theoretical techniques for measuring kinetic jet power. There is also an ongoing effort to compare and contrast jet power measurement techniques, to elucidate important AGN parameters such as jet composition and entrainment of external material by the radio lobes. Understanding jet physics will go a long way to helping interpret existing and future large-scale AGN surveys such as ATLAS and EMU. Accurate measurement of AGN kinetic power is necessary for quantifying the total AGN energy budget, and the impact of AGN on the formation and evolution of galaxies.
