About Me

I am a reformed astrophysicist, and for the past 2 and a half years I have been working as a data scientist/consultant with Teradata, a leading database and big data analytics provider. As a data scientist, my role is to perform advanced analytics and machine learning in a variety of subject matter domains, addressing a diverse array of analytics use-cases including anomaly detection, causal analysis, graph analysis and predictive modeling. This work is typically performed using a combination of Python, Shell scripting, SQL, as well as data visualisation tools such as Tableau for business intelligence applications.

Prior to joining Teradata, I was a post-doctoral research fellow at ASTRON, the Netherlands Institute for Radio Astronomy, near Dwingeloo in the northern Netherlands. I previously held a post-doctoral position at the International Centre for Radio Astronomy Research (ICRAR), Curtin University, in Perth, Western Australia.

My academic research was mainly focused on studying the astrophysical processes of active galaxies using the techniques of radio and X-ray astronomy. I took a strong interest in the international Square Kilometre Array (SKA) project, and have written a comprehensive review detailing the science case for the long baseline component.

I graduated with a PhD from the Australian National University, Mount Stromlo Observatory, in 2009. My thesis was titled "Multi-wavelength studies of X-ray bright Extragalactic Jets", and was supervised by Geoff Bicknell, Jim Lovell and Dave Jauncey.
